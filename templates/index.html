Welcome to pkmntcgdb

This site is dedicated to catalog every english pokemon tcg card edition ever made, with corresponding card image or scan.

Along with providing the most detailed pokemon card edition database, it easily allows you to keep track of your card collection.

How it works?

The site provides a searching field, where you can search by pokemon name, number or expansion set.

Say, you found a hidden Azumarill card from steam siege expansion set under your bed and you want to add it to your collection! Easy!

The website searches through the database to find the corresponding Pokemon based on your query.

You'll see something like this:

On the top you see the card number, name and set it belongs to.
Under the card, you can see the list of editions this card came in.

(Psst... you can click the Pokeball under the card image to switch the card edition images!)

After you create a free user account, you can add this card to your collection!

You can also have multiple collections per account - this might come in handy, for example if you want to keep track of the cards you have for trade, or to catalog the cards per binder!

The site then keeps track of the cards you have, and provides simple charts showing your completionist progress, if you're into that.

Say we have the listed Azumarill in reverse holo edition, and some normal cards too. Here's how a steam siege progress chart might look like.

{% include 'widgets/single_chart.jinja2' %}

You can see there are lines for Normal and Reverse holo editions, but also a line for Set cards.

The Set cards line shows the number of cards you have from the set, regardless of the edition.

You can also share a link to your account to post to your friends and show your collection to them!

There's also a detailed view of one expansion set with checkboxes on your public page.

But ... why?

I've come across my own collection of pokemon cards I've collected when I was young - and being a techie and such, I wanted to catalog my collection online. I've looked left and right, and I couldn't find any solid existing solution - either the app missed reverse holos, or it didn't track multiple copies, or it was just super difficult to use.
